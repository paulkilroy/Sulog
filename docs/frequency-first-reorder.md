# Frequency-first reorder — old vs new (draft)

**Status:** draft proposal, 2026-06-22. A principled frequency-first reordering
of Sulog's *existing* curriculum, to compare against the current source-driven
order. The lesson/unit **engine stays**; only the *order/placement* changes.

**Confidence:** The top tier (grammatical core — markers, pronouns,
demonstratives, particles, negators, question words) is **high confidence** —
these dominate the top of any Waray frequency list and are linguistically
stable. Content-word placement (verbs, nouns, themes) is a **reasonable
estimate** pending the CHED top-1000 ranks (`docs/sources/waray-frequency-sources.md`).

---

## The headline

Sulog currently teaches **all the grammatical glue in Section 3 ("Building
Blocks")** — *after* two whole sections of greetings, people, jobs, and
describing. But that glue (an, hin, han, ha, ngan; ako/ka/hiya; ini/iton/adto;
na/pa/la/ba; diri/waray; ano/hain) is **the single highest-frequency material in
the language** — it appears in essentially every sentence.

**Frequency-first flips this: the grammar core moves to the very front and gets
interleaved from day one.** Thematic vocab (jobs, church, airport, cooking,
months) moves later.

would make the bulk of Section 3 into **Phase 1**.

## Proposed frequency-first Phase 1 (what "day one" becomes)

A learner who knows just this can build real sentences immediately:

1. **Yes/no & courtesy** — oo, diri, waray, salamat, maupay, pasensya, sige, pwede
2. **Greetings** — Maupay nga aga/udto/kulop/gab-i, Kumusta ka?, Maupay man
3. **Core pronouns** — ako, ka/ikaw, hiya, kita, kami, kamo, hira
4. **The big markers** — hi, an/it, hin/han, ha, ngan (taught as sentence glue, not a chart)
5. **This/that** — ini, iton, adto
6. **Have / there is / none** — may, mayda, waray, adi
7. **First questions** — Ano ini?, Hain...?, Hin-o...?, Kumusta?
8. **Numbers 1–10**
9. **Immediate family** — tatay, nanay, anak, bugto

vs. today's Phase-1 equivalent (S1), which is greetings + yes/no only, then makes
you wait until Section 3 for the words that actually wire sentences together.
