## Lesson 1 — Pronouns & equational sentences (pp.1–2)
Grammar: two kinds of pronouns — Personal (I-class) and General (this/that/here…).
Three classes of personal pronouns (I, II, III). I-class marks the TOPIC.
Equational sentences have no verb (noun/adjective "equated" with pronoun).
`mga` = pluralizer before a noun.

I-class personal pronouns: ako (I), ikaw/ka (you), hiya (he/she),
kita (we incl), kami (we excl), kamo (you pl), hira (they).

Vocab/examples: Pilipino ka, Amerikano ako, Estudyante hiya, Mahusay hiya,
Hataas ako, Riko hira, Mga lalake kita, Mga babaye kami, Mga sangkay kita,
Mga Pilipino kami; makusog (strong), gwapo (handsome), matambok (fat),
mapaso (hot), misyonaryo (missionary), maestro (teacher), lalake (man),
babaye (woman), Kristohanon (Christian).

**Accent marks (p.3) — stress key:** ´ = stressed syllable; ` = unstressed + glottal
stop; ^ = stressed + glottal stop. Stress-marked vocab (use to fix our phonetics):
Tátay, Nánay, Amerikáno, Asáwa, Babáye, Buótan, Ríko, Estudyánte, Mahúsay,
Gwápo, Sángkay, mapasó, Kristohánon, maduróto (industrious), makusóg, laláke,
maéstro, Misyonáryo, Pilípino, Hatáas, Matámbok.

## Lesson 2 — I-Class markers (pp.4–5)
Markers (mark the TOPIC): proper nouns → **hi** (sg) / **hira** (pl);
common nouns → **an** (sg) / **an mga** (pl). **ngan** = "and" (connector).
Examples: Ako hi Peter (I am Peter); Hiya hi Bebing; Pilipino hi Pablo;
Hira Perla ngan Tessie (They are Perla and Tessie); Buotan hi Ernesto;
Riko hira Marvin ngan Annie; Mahusay an lamesa (the table is nice);
Mabusag an papel (the paper is white); Malabad an mga bata (the children are
naughty); Madig-on an mga lingkuran (the chairs are strong/sturdy).
New vocab: lamesa (table), papel (paper), busag (white), bata (child),
lingkuran (chair), malabad (naughty), madig-on (sturdy).
L2 vocab box (pp.6–7): lingkúran (chair), lamésa (table), báso (glass),
léksyon (lesson), batá (child/baby), madig-on (strong-things), bóla (ball),
malidong (round), malábad (naughty), makurí (difficult), dakú (big),
maúpay (good), gutíay (small), mahúsay (pretty), kláse (class), papél (paper),
punô (full), mabuság (white), masayón (easy). Examples: Daku an klase (the class
is big), Puno an baso (the glass is full).
