## Lesson 1 — Pronouns & equational sentences (pp.1–2)
Grammar: two kinds of pronouns — Personal (I-class) and General (this/that/here…).
Three classes of personal pronouns (I, II, III). I-class marks the TOPIC.
Equational sentences have no verb (noun/adjective "equated" with pronoun).
`mga` = pluralizer before a noun.

I-class personal pronouns: ako (I), ikaw/ka (you), hiya (he/she),
kita (we incl), kami (we excl), kamo (you pl), hira (they).

Vocab/examples: Pilipino ka, Amerikano ako, Estudyante hiya, Mahusay hiya,
Hataas ako, Riko hira, Mga lalake kita, Mga babaye kami, Mga sangkay kita,
Mga Pilipino kami; makusog (strong), gwapo (handsome), matambok (fat),
mapaso (hot), misyonaryo (missionary), maestro (teacher), lalake (man),
babaye (woman), Kristohanon (Christian).

**Accent marks (p.3) — stress key:** ´ = stressed syllable; ` = unstressed + glottal
stop; ^ = stressed + glottal stop. Stress-marked vocab (use to fix our phonetics):
Tátay, Nánay, Amerikáno, Asáwa, Babáye, Buótan, Ríko, Estudyánte, Mahúsay,
Gwápo, Sángkay, mapasó, Kristohánon, maduróto (industrious), makusóg, laláke,
maéstro, Misyonáryo, Pilípino, Hatáas, Matámbok.

## Lesson 2 — I-Class markers (pp.4–5)
Markers (mark the TOPIC): proper nouns → **hi** (sg) / **hira** (pl);
common nouns → **an** (sg) / **an mga** (pl). **ngan** = "and" (connector).
Examples: Ako hi Peter (I am Peter); Hiya hi Bebing; Pilipino hi Pablo;
Hira Perla ngan Tessie (They are Perla and Tessie); Buotan hi Ernesto;
Riko hira Marvin ngan Annie; Mahusay an lamesa (the table is nice);
Mabusag an papel (the paper is white); Malabad an mga bata (the children are
naughty); Madig-on an mga lingkuran (the chairs are strong/sturdy).
New vocab: lamesa (table), papel (paper), busag (white), bata (child),
lingkuran (chair), malabad (naughty), madig-on (sturdy).
L2 vocab box (pp.6–7): lingkúran (chair), lamésa (table), báso (glass),
léksyon (lesson), batá (child/baby), madig-on (strong-things), bóla (ball),
malidong (round), malábad (naughty), makurí (difficult), dakú (big),
maúpay (good), gutíay (small), mahúsay (pretty), kláse (class), papél (paper),
punô (full), mabuság (white), masayón (easy). Examples: Daku an klase (the class
is big), Puno an baso (the glass is full).

## Lesson 3 — Demonstratives (general pronouns) + nga linker (pp.8–10)
Grammar: "g" inserted in first syllable for plural adjective (hataas → hagtaas).
**I-Class General Pronouns (demonstratives):** `ini` = this (very near speaker);
`iton`/`ito` = that (near speaker); `adto` = that "over there" (far). Plural uses
`mga`. Emphasis: demonstrative + marker → `Ini an tubig` (This is the water).
**nga linker** joins a demonstrative to a noun: `ini nga babaye` (this woman),
`iton nga lalake` (that man), `adto nga bata` (that child over there).
Examples: Tubig ini (this is water); Lamesa iton; Kahoy adto (that's a tree);
Tawo iton (that's a person); Dagat iton (that's the sea); Mga libro ini (these
are books); Mata ini (this is an eye); Maraut ini nga tawo (this is a bad person).
New vocab: kahoy (tree), tawo (person), dagat (sea), libro (book),
problema (problem), lapis (pencil), simbahan (church), mata (eye), maraut (bad).

L3 vocab box (p.11): ádlaw (day/sun), káhoy (tree/lumber), bag-o (new),
kwárto (room), matúgnaw (cold-thing), lápis (pencil), dáan (old), líbro (book),
dágat (sea), matá (eye), dámo (many), habubô (short), karsáda (road),
probléma (problem), maráut (bad), simbáhan (church), dyip (jeepney),
táwo (person), útan (cooked vegetable), túbig (water).

## Lesson 4 — II-Class pronouns (possessives) & II-Class markers (pp.12–~15)
**II-Class personal pronouns = POSSESSOR (attached to a noun):**
nakon/ko (my), nimo/mo (your sg), niya (his/her), naton (our incl),
namon (our excl), nyo (your pl), nira (their).
Examples: libro nakon (my book), lapis nimo (your pencil), uyab niya (his/her
sweetheart). [II-Class markers continue on following pages.]

## Lessons 5–7 — Verbal sentences & the ma- (actor focus) verb system
- **L5 (p.16)** Verbal sentences / Focuses & ma- Actor Focus. Unlike equational
  sentences, verbal sentences have a verb + actor (+ object/location/beneficiary/
  instrument). "Verb focuses" mark which part is the topic by raising the voice;
  Waray uses different verb forms (focuses).
- **L6 (p.20)** ma- Actor Focus w/ Object & Location. Formula:
  `ma-Verb + I-Cl actor + II-Cl object + III-Cl L/D/B`. E.g. *Mapalit ako hin
  saging ha tindahan* (I'll buy a banana at the store). New: saging (banana),
  tindahan (store), eskwelahan (school).
- **L7 (p.24)** ma- Actor Focus w/ Location & Beneficiary (III-Cl general pronouns
  & markers han/hin/ha). L7 vocab (p.28): tsinelas (slippers), apó (grandchildren),
  bukád (flower), estórya (story), gátas (milk), kanta (to sing), dara (to bring),
  kapé (coffee), pamílya (family), pára (for/on behalf of), pinirito nga manok
  (fried chicken), labay (to pass by), bado (dress).

## Full course arc (whole 114pp scanned by sampling)
It's a **grammar-progression** course (very different from Sulog's theme-first
decks). The backbone, in order:
1. **L1–4** Pronoun system: I-class personal pronouns + equational sentences;
   I-class markers (hi/hira, an/an mga, ngan); demonstratives (ini/iton/adto +
   nga linker); II-class **possessive** pronouns (nakon/ko, nimo/mo, niya, naton,
   namon, nyo, nira).
2. **L8–9** III-class pronouns: standalone possessives (akon/imo/iya/aton/amon/
   iyo/ira = "mine/yours…") and the **ha + pronoun** prepositional forms (ha akon
   = to/at/with/from me).
3. **~L10–12** Yes/no questions with **ba** (Isda ba ini? = Is this fish?).
4. **L5–7 & ~L24** Verb **focuses**: ma- actor focus (+object/location/
   beneficiary, III-cl markers han/hin/ha) and mag- verbs.
5. **~L16** Intensifiers & particles: **hin duro** (very), **liwat** (also),
   **na** (already), **pa** (still).
6. **~L20** Negation: **waray** (didn't/none) + **diri** (not) with particles.
7. **~L28** Question words: **hin-o** (who) + the **man** particle (and ano/hain…).
Scattered thematic vocab to harvest: saging (banana), tindahan (store),
eskwelahan (school), kahoy (tree), dagat (sea), libro (book), tubig (water),
gatas (milk), kape (coffee), pamilya (family), tinapay (bread), mangga (mango),
sabaw (soup), kahoy, balaod (law), Ginoo (Lord), uma (farm), kabulig (helper).

## PROPOSED Sulog structure from this course
Add a grammar-driven section (the app's biggest gap), vocab/forms-first then
sentences, 8–10 items/lesson:
**Section "Building Blocks"** (grammar):
- Unit *My & your* — II-class possessives: nakon/ko, nimo/mo, niya, naton, namon,
  nyo, nira; then phrases (libro nakon, lapis nimo…).
- Unit *Mine & yours* — III-class: akon/imo/iya/aton/amon/iyo/ira; ha+pronoun
  (ha akon…); then phrases (Akon ini, Akon ini nga balay…).
- Unit *This & that* — ini/iton/adto, + nga linker (already partly in app's
  "Describing sentences"; extend with demonstratives).
- Unit *Markers* — hi/si, an/an mga, hin/han, ha, ngan; then tagged sentences.
- Unit *Asking* — question words: hin-o (who), ano (what), hain/diin (where),
  san-o (when), kay ano (why), mapira/tagpira (how many/much), + ba yes/no.
- Unit *Little words* — particles: na (already), pa (still), liwat (also),
  hin duro (very); negation waray/diri.
- Unit *Verb focuses* — extend "Verbs in action": ma- / mag- / nag- with
  actor+object+location examples.
NOTE: phonetics for new grammar words are best-effort; the course's accent marks
(p.3 key) give stress for many (use them). Teacher pass recommended.
